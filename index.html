Where I'm From

I grew up in Plainfield, Indiana, having gone to Avon High School. I then went to Indiana University, where I got my Bachelor's and Master's degrees. I now live in a suburb of Indianapolis, Indiana, where I work at Eli Lilly and Company as a Solutions Architect. While having spent my life in Indiana, I have traveled to many places, including many other states, Israel, and doing an internship in Australia for a summer.

What are my passions?

1. Faith. My faith is the most important thing in my life. I strive to live my life in a way that lives out my faith and its foundational principles.
2. People. This is number one because my greatest passion is to pour into people and help them become the best version of themselves.
3. Nature. I love being outside. The beauty of nature is second to none and feeling the breeze as I walk through the wonder of nature gives me more life than anything else.
4. Learning. I love learning new things. I love to build as I often see the results of what I am learning in a very tangible way. This can be building websites, woodworking, or even just some yard work.
5. Family. Family is what gets me out of bed in the morning. I wake up every day to my favorite people.

What are my goals?

1. I strive to honor G-d with my life.
2. I strive to live out my family's guiding principals.
3. I strive to be the best husband and father I can be.
4. I strive to be a leader who pours into others and helps them grow.
5. I strive to be a person who is known for their integrity and honesty.
6. I strive to be patient and kind, quick to listen, and empathetic.
7. I strive to use my life to truly help as many people as I can.

Information Systems

This degree focused mostly on technological foundations. This includes things such as getting intimately familiar with Excel, understanding databases and SQL, learning some fundamental programming, how to tranlsate business needs into user stories and technical requirements, and how internet and infrastructure works.

Management

This degree focused on people. This ranged from the nuiances of international business, to a thorough understanding of HR and its functions, and included things such as change management and organizational structures.

Business Law, Ethics, and Decision Making

This degree focused on understanding the legal and ethical implications of business decisions. This includes things such as contract law, tort law, and the different ethical frameworks that can be used to make decisions.

Cybersecurity

This concentration was dedicated to preparing students to pass the CISSP exam, excluding vocabulary. It was all about concepts: the fundamentals of cybersecurity, how hackers work, understanding encryption, and the legality around cybersecurity.

Business Intelligence and Analytics

This concentration was dedicated to understanding and building supervised learning models and how to make decisions based on the results of those models. It also dug into unsupervised learning models, providing a foundation and understanding to move into the AI world.

Enterprise Resource Planning

This concentration was dedicated to understanding how ERP systems work. It specialized in understanding how SAP works and demonstrating our knowledge by building a system from Company Code up. Twice.

Hiking

Hiking is one of my most life-giving hobbies. Being outside in forests, creeks, or anywhere else that I can bask in the beauty of nature is my happy place. It really is as simple as that.

Fishing

I find fishing to be either medatative or an excellent bonding experience. I can sit there in silence for hours, enjoying the sun and the water, or I can be with friends and family, laughing and joking as we wait on the next bite. Some of the best days fishing happen even when you don't catch anything.

Photography

Photography is not just a creative outlet for me. It is an opportunity to learn and master new skills with a camera, drone, and editing software. I love the fact that it quite literally encourages me to look at things from different perspectives and challenges me to look beyond the initial appearance. It helps give me a tangible reminder of the depth that lies just under the surface and the power of intentionality.

Writing

Writing is a chance for me to get my thoughts down. These may take the form of a journal, a written prayer, an essay about something I am passionate about, or even a fictional story. I love the opportunity to process my thoughts and then wrestle them into something cohesive, concise, and comprehensible.

Reading

Reading is a chance for me to learn and grow as well as an opportunity to relax and escape. I have found that I am able to do all four reading or listening to fiction and non-fiction. I find fiction has lessons that it teaches through its world and characters and non-fiction can create a space for me to lean into and embrace as it creates illustrations of the past and lessons we can learn.

SCUBA Diving

SCUBA diving feels like home. I love the water and I always have. Being submerged and witnessing the beauty of underwater life is a gift. If I had enough air, I could stay down for hours, just watching the ecosystem around me. I love seeing the fish exhibit their natural behaviors and, much like hiking, basking in the beauty of nature.
